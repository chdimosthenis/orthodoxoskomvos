The **Church of Jerusalem** is the "Mother of all Churches" of all of Christendom, because it was in Jerusalem that the Church was established on the day of [Pentecost](/Pentecost "Pentecost") with the descent of the [Holy Spirit](/Holy_Spirit "Holy Spirit") on the disciples of [Jesus Christ](/Jesus_Christ "Jesus Christ"). From Jerusalem the gospel of Christ was spread to the world.

As Christianity spread, and the persecutions of the Jews by Roman authorities in their homeland increased, causing the dispersion of many of the Christians from Jerusalem, the import of this church and its impact on the ongoing life of the whole Church diminished. As other churches gained ascendency, namely the Churches of [Rome](/Church_of_Rome "Church of Rome"), [Constantinople](/Church_of_Constantinople "Church of Constantinople"), [Alexandria](/Church_of_Alexandria "Church of Alexandria"), and [Antioch](/Church_of_Antioch "Church of Antioch"), the Church of Jerusalem was accorded a place of honor with them among the five original Christian [patriarchates](/Patriarchate "Patriarchate") of the Christian world, called the *[Pentarchy](/Pentarchy "Pentarchy")*.

The Church of Jerusalem remains the custodian of many of the holy sites in Jerusalem and environs, sometimes jointly with [Roman Catholic](/Roman_Catholic_Church "Roman Catholic Church") or [Coptic](/Church_of_Alexandria_(Coptic) "Church of Alexandria (Coptic)") or [Armenian](/Church_of_Armenia "Church of Armenia") Christians, including the [Church of the Holy Sepulchre](/Church_of_the_Holy_Sepulchre_(Jerusalem) "Church of the Holy Sepulchre (Jerusalem)") in Jerusalem and the [Church of the Nativity](/Church_of_the_Nativity_(Bethlehem) "Church of the Nativity (Bethlehem)") in Bethlehem.

Lately there has been criticism of the church leadership by Palestinian faithful, who accuse the Greek-speaking and largely Greek-born leadership of squandering their money and treating their Arabic-speaking members as second-class faithful.

Following the [deposition](/Deposition "Deposition") of Patriarch [Irenaios I](/Irenaios_I_(Skopelitis)_of_Jerusalem "Irenaios I (Skopelitis) of Jerusalem") amidst scandals regarding the transfer of land to Jewish control, the Holy Synod of the church named as their [temporary primate](/Locum_tenens "Locum tenens") His Eminence Metropolitan [Cornelius (Rodousakis) of Petra](/Cornelius_(Rodousakis)_of_Petra "Cornelius (Rodousakis) of Petra"). On [August 22](/August_22 "August 22"), 2005, the Holy Synod unanimously elected the former Archbishop of Tabor, [Theophilus](/Theophilus_III_(Giannopoulos)_of_Jerusalem "Theophilus III (Giannopoulos) of Jerusalem"), as the 141st Patriarch of Jerusalem.

## History

The first Apostolic Council was held in ca. 49-52AD and ruled that the Gentiles do not have to become Jews before becoming Christians.

In 70 AD, the Roman Emperor [Titus](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titus "w:Titus") captured and destroyed Jerusalem. The Jewish temple was demolished, and, under difficult conditions, the Christians moved to Pella on the east bank of the Jordan River.

By 135 AD, the Roman Emperor [Hadrian](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadrian "w:Hadrian") built a pagan temple over the ruins of Golgotha and covered the Holy Tomb with dirt. A new Roman city was declared and named *Aelia Capitolina*. Although Christians were then permitted to return to Jerusalem, the Jewish people were not. In the meantime, Christianity spread throughout Palestine; many communities and episcopacies were created under the primacy of the Metropolitan of Caesaria.

By 326 AD, St. Helena visited Jerusalem to rediscover the ruins of the old Jerusalem. During the excavations, she discovered three crosses, and through a miraculous event the [True Cross](/True_Cross "True Cross") of Christ was discovered. Her son, St. [Constantine the Great](/Constantine_the_Great "Constantine the Great") commissioned the demolition of the Roman buildings and the construction of the [Church of the Holy Sepulchre](/Church_of_the_Holy_Sepulchre_(Jerusalem) "Church of the Holy Sepulchre (Jerusalem)").

The chapel of the Forty Holy Martyrs holds the relics of all the patriarchs of Jerusalem.

## Saints and Elders

The [Holy Land](/Holy_Land "Holy Land") has been primarily sanctified by the presence of [Christ](/Christ "Christ"), however, through his divine economy, the Holy Land has also generated a cloud of [Martyrs](/Martyrs "Martyrs"), Hierarchs and [Saints](/Saints "Saints"). Some of these include:

* the Holy Apostles
* St. [Sabbas the Sanctified](/Sabbas_the_Sanctified "Sabbas the Sanctified") of Palestine ([December 5](/December_5 "December 5"))
