The **Third Ecumenical Council** was held in Ephesus, Asia Minor, in 431 under Emperor [Theodosius II](/Theodosius_II "Theodosius II"), grandson of [Theodosius the Great](/Theodosius_I "Theodosius I"). It is also known as the **Council of Ephesus**. Approximately 200 [bishops](/Bishop "Bishop") were present, though procedings began in haste before the arrival of the bishops from the west. The procedings were conducted in a heated atmosphere of confrontation and recriminations. It was the third of the [Ecumenical Councils](/Ecumenical_Councils "Ecumenical Councils"), and was chiefly concerned with [Nestorianism](/Nestorianism "Nestorianism").

## Christological Controversies

According to the Council, Nestorianism overemphasized the human nature of [Christ](/Jesus_Christ "Jesus Christ") at the expense of the divine. The Council denounced Patriarch [Nestorius](/Nestorius "Nestorius")' teaching as erroneous. Nestorius taught that the Virgin Mary gave birth to a man, [Jesus Christ](/Jesus_Christ "Jesus Christ"), not God the [Logos](/Logos "Logos"). The Logos only dwelt in Christ, as in a Temple (Christ, therefore, was only *Theophoros*: the "Bearer of God.") Consequently, the [Virgin Mary](/Virgin_Mary "Virgin Mary") should be called *Christotokos* ("Mother of Christ") and not *[Theotokos](/Theotokos "Theotokos")* ("Birth-giver of God").

The Council decreed that Christ was one person, not two separate "people": fully God and fully man, with a rational soul and body. The Virgin Mary is *[Theotokos](/Theotokos "Theotokos")* because she gave birth not to a mere man but to God as a man. The [union of the two natures](/index.php?title=Theandric_union&action=edit&redlink=1 "Theandric union (page does not exist)") of Christ took place in such a fashion that one did not disturb the other.

The Council also declared the text of the [Nicene-Constantinopolitan Creed](/Nicene-Constantinopolitan_Creed "Nicene-Constantinopolitan Creed") to be complete and forbade any additional change to it. In addition, it condemned [Pelagianism](/Pelagianism "Pelagianism").

## Additional significance

The [autocephaly](/Autocephaly "Autocephaly") of the [Church of Cyprus](/Church_of_Cyprus "Church of Cyprus") was established, overriding claims by the Patriarch of Antioch.

## Commemoration

The Holy Fathers of the Third Ecumenical Council are commemorated on [September 9](/September_9 "September 9") and also on the 9th Sunday after [Pentecost](/Pentecost "Pentecost") the [Sunday of the Fathers of the First Six Councils](/Sunday_of_the_Fathers_of_the_First_Six_Councils "Sunday of the Fathers of the First Six Councils").
