Our venerable and God-bearing father **Mark the Ascetic** was born in Athens in the fifth century, and lived in the Egyptian desert as a [monk](/Monk "Monk"). His [feast day](/Feast_day "Feast day") is commemorated on [March 5](/March_5 "March 5").

## Life

St. Mark was an [ascetic](/Ascetic "Ascetic") and [miracle-worker](/Wonder-worker "Wonder-worker"), sometimes known as *Mark the Faster*. In his 40th year he was [tonsured](/Tonsure "Tonsure") a monk by his teacher, St. [John Chrysostom](/John_Chrysostom "John Chrysostom"). Mark then spent 60 more years in the wilderness of Nitria (a desert in Lower Egypt) in [fasting](/Fast "Fast") and [prayer](/Prayer "Prayer"), and in writing many spiritual works concerning the salvation of souls. He knew all the [Holy Scriptures](/Holy_Scriptures "Holy Scriptures") by heart. He was very merciful and kind, and wept much for the misfortunes that had befallen all of God's creation.

On one occasion, when weeping over a hyena's blind whelp, he prayed to God and the whelp received its sight. In thanksgiving the mother hyena brought him a sheepskin. The [saint](/Saint "Saint") forbade the hyena in the future to kill any more sheep belonging to poor people. He received [Communion](/Eucharist "Eucharist") at the hands of [angels](/Angels "Angels"). His [homilies](/Homily "Homily") concerned such topics as the spiritual law, repentance, sobriety, and are ranked among the preeminent literature of the Church. These works were praised by the [Patriarch](/Patriarch "Patriarch") [Photius the Great](/Photius_the_Great "Photius the Great") himself.

## Quotes

"Do not seek the perfection of the law in human virtues, for it is not found perfect in them. Its perfection is hidden in the Cross of Christ."
