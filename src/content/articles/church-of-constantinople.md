The **Church of Constantinople** is one of the fourteen or fifteen [autocephalous](/Autocephaly "Autocephaly") churches, also referred to as the **Ecumenical [Patriarchate](/Patriarchate "Patriarchate")**. It is headed by the Ecumenical Patriarch, who has the status of *[primus inter pares](/Primus_inter_pares "Primus inter pares")* ("first among equals") among the world's Orthodox [bishops](/Bishop "Bishop"). The local churches of the Ecumenical Patriarchate consist of five archdioceses, three churches, thirteen metropolises, and one diocese, each of which reports directly to the Patriarch of Constantinople with no intervening authority. In addition, three of the five archdioceses have internal metropolises (16 in all), which are part of their respective archdioceses rather than distinct administrative entities, unlike the other metropolises.

The current Ecumenical Patriarch is His All-Holiness [Bartholomew I](/Bartholomew_I_(Archontonis)_of_Constantinople "Bartholomew I (Archontonis) of Constantinople"), Archbishop of Constantinople.

## Peculiar prerogatives of the patriarchate

*Main article: [Prerogatives of the Ecumenical Patriarchate](/Prerogatives_of_the_Ecumenical_Patriarchate "Prerogatives of the Ecumenical Patriarchate")*

In history and in [canonical literature](/Canons_(law) "Canons (law)") (i.e. the Church's canons and traditional commentaries on them), the Ecumenical Patriarchate has been granted certain prerogatives (*[presbeia](/Presbeia "Presbeia")*) which other autocephalous Orthodox churches do not have. Not all of these prerogatives are today universally acknowledged, though all do have precedents in history and canonical references. The nature of these prerogatives or even their very existence is hotly contested on [canonical grounds](/Prerogatives_of_the_Ecumenical_Patriarchate#Canonical_claims "Prerogatives of the Ecumenical Patriarchate") by certain other Orthodox churches, particularly the [Church of Russia](/Church_of_Russia "Church of Russia").

The following is a (non-exhaustive) list of these prerogatives and their reference points:

* Equal prerogatives to Old Rome (Canon 3 of the [Second Ecumenical Council](/Second_Ecumenical_Council "Second Ecumenical Council"), Canon 28 of the [Fourth Ecumenical Council](/Fourth_Ecumenical_Council "Fourth Ecumenical Council"), Canon 36 of the [Quinisext Council](/Quinisext_Council "Quinisext Council"))
* The right to hear appeals, if invited, regarding disputes between clergy (Canons 9 and 17 of the Fourth Ecumenical Council)
* The right to ordain bishops for areas outside defined [canonical boundaries](/Canonical_territory "Canonical territory") (Canon 28 of the Fourth Ecumenical Council)
* The right to establish [stavropegial](/Stavropegial "Stavropegial") [monasteries](/Monastery "Monastery") even in the territories of other [patriarchates](/Patriarchate "Patriarchate") (the [Epanagoge](/index.php?title=Epanagoge&action=edit&redlink=1 "Epanagoge (page does not exist)"), commentaries of [Matthew Blastares](/Matthew_Blastares "Matthew Blastares") and [Theodore Balsamon](/index.php?title=Theodore_Balsamon&action=edit&redlink=1 "Theodore Balsamon (page does not exist)"))
