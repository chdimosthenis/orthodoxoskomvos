Our father among the [saints](/Saint "Saint") **Cyril of Alexandria** was the [Pope](/Pope "Pope") of Alexandria at the time Alexandria was at its height in influence and power within the Roman Empire. Cyril wrote extensively and was a leading protagonist in the [Christological](/Christology "Christology") controversies of the 4th and 5th centuries. He was a central figure in the [Council of Ephesus](/Third_Ecumenical_Council "Third Ecumenical Council") in 431 which led to the [deposition](/Deposition "Deposition") of [Nestorius](/Nestorius "Nestorius") as Archbishop of Constantinople. Cyril is among the patristic fathers, and his reputation within the Orthodox Christian world has led to his acquiring the title "Seal of all the Fathers." His [feast day](/Feast_day "Feast day") is celebrated on [June 9](/June_9 "June 9") and, with St. [Athanasius of Alexandria](/Athanasius_of_Alexandria "Athanasius of Alexandria"), on [January 18](/January_18 "January 18").

## Life

Cyril was born about 378 in the small town of Theodosios, Egypt, near modern day Malalla el Kobra. His mother's brother, [Theophilus](/Theophilus_of_Alexandria "Theophilus of Alexandria"), was a [priest](/Priest "Priest") who rose to the powerful position of Pope of Alexandria. His mother remained close to her brother and under his guidance Cyril was well educated. His education showed through his knowledge, in his writings, of Christian writers of his day, including [Eusebius](/Eusebius_of_Caesarea "Eusebius of Caesarea"), [Origen](/Origen "Origen"), [Didymus](/Didymus_the_Blind "Didymus the Blind"), and writers of the Alexandrian church. He showed a knowledge of Latin through his extensive correspondence with the [Bishop](/Bishop "Bishop") of Rome, [Pope](/Pope "Pope") Celestine. His formal education appeared normal for his day: 390-392 grammatical studies at ages 12 to 14, 393-397 Rhetoric/Humanities at ages 15 to 20, and 398-402 Christian theology and biblical studies.

He was [tonsured](/Tonsure "Tonsure") a [reader](/Reader "Reader") by his uncle, Theophilus, in the [Church of Alexandria](/Church_of_Alexandria "Church of Alexandria") and under his uncle's guidance advanced in knowledge and position. He supported his uncle in the removal of St. [John Chrysostom](/John_Chrysostom "John Chrysostom") as [archbishop](/Archbishop "Archbishop") of Constantinople, although this was justified as an administrative, not doctrinal, issue, as later Cyril supported John's return as when he contrasted Nestorius' unorthodoxy to Chrysostom's purity of doctrine to the imperial court.

Theophilus died on [October 15](/October_15 "October 15"), 412, and Cyril was made pope on [October 18](/October_18 "October 18"), 412, over stiff opposition by the party for the incumbent [Archdeacon](/Archdeacon "Archdeacon") Timothy in a volatile Alexandrian atmosphere. Thus, Cyril followed first Athanasius and then Theophilus as the Pope of Alexandria in the position that had become powerful and influential, rivaling that of the city Prefect.

His early years as pope were caught up in the problems of a cosmopolitan city where the animosities among the various Christian factions, [Jews](/Judaism "Judaism"), and pagans brought frequent violence. In addition, there was the rivalry between Alexandria and Constantinople and a clash between [Alexandrian](/index.php?title=Alexandrian_school&action=edit&redlink=1 "Alexandrian school (page does not exist)") and [Antiochian](/index.php?title=Antiochian_school&action=edit&redlink=1 "Antiochian school (page does not exist)") schools of ecclesiastical reflection, piety, and discourse. These issues came to a head in 428 when the [see](/See "See") of Constantinople became vacant. Nestorius, from the Antiochian party, was made Archbishop of Constantinople on [April 10](/April_10 "April 10"), 428, and stoked the fires by denouncing the use of the term *[Theotokos](/Theotokos "Theotokos")* as not a proper rendition of Mary's position in relation to [Christ](/Christ "Christ").

Thus, Cyril and the Alexandrian party crossed swords with those of the Antiochian party in the imperial home court. After much in-fighting, Augusta [Pulcheria](/Pulcheria_the_Empress "Pulcheria the Empress"), older sister of the Emperor Theodosius II, sided with Cyril against Nestorius. To rid himself of Cyril, Nestorius recommended to the emperor a [council](/Ecumenical_Councils "Ecumenical Councils") in Constantinople. But, when Theodosius called the council it was in Ephesus, an area friendly to Cyril. After months of manuevering the [Council of 431](/Third_Ecumenical_Council "Third Ecumenical Council") ended with Nestorius being removed from office and sent into exile.

Cyril died on [June 27](/June_27 "June 27"), 444, but the controversies were to continue for decades, from the [Robber Council of Ephesus](/Robber_Council_of_Ephesus "Robber Council of Ephesus") in 449 to the [Council of Chalcedon](/Fourth_Ecumenical_Council "Fourth Ecumenical Council") in 451 and beyond.

## Legacy

As noted above, Cyril was a scholarly archbishop and a prolific writer. In the early years of his active life in the Church he wrote several exegeses. Among these were: *Commentaries on the [Old Testament](/Old_Testament "Old Testament")*, *Thesaurus*, *Discourse Against [Arians](/Arianism "Arianism")*, *Commentary on [St. John's Gospel](/Gospel_of_John "Gospel of John")*, and *Dialogues on the [Trinity](/Trinity "Trinity")*. In 429 as the Christological controversies increased, his output of writings was that which his opponents could not match. His writings and his theology have remained central to tradition of the Fathers and to all Orthodox even up today.
